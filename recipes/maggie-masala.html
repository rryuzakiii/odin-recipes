maggie masala

In 1869, Julius Maggi (1846-1912) took over his father's mill business in Kemptthal, Switzerland. Under his leadership, the business developed into one of the pioneers of industrial food production, with the aim of improving the diet of working-class families through better nutrient supply and faster preparation

In 1882, at a meeting of the Swiss "Common Good Society" (Gemeinnützigen Gesellschaft), the doctor and factory inspector Fridolin Schuler spoke about the miserable nutritional situation of the factory workers: women workers no longer had enough time to cook for their families; cold meals or alcohol often replaced warm meals; meals were served in factory canteens and were cheap but not sufficiently nutritious. The consequences were malnutrition, stomach diseases, and high infant mortality. Schuler advocated for diets based on high-protein, easily digestible pulses/legumes. He demanded that such meals should be offered to the working class in a convenient form for quick preparation and at a low price. The society turned to the Maggi company, among others.

Ingredients

  • 1 pack of maggi nooles
  • 1 and half cups of water
  • 1 tsp oil
  • 1 and half onion , finely chopped
  • 1 tomato ,finely chopped
  • 1 green chili , finely chopped (opitonal)
  • 1 tsp ginger - garlic paste
  • 1 tsp turmic powder
  • 1tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp gram masala
  • half tsp coriander powder
  • salt to taste
  • fresh coriander leaves ,chopped for garnish

Maggie Masala reciepe:

  • Boil 1 ½ cups of water, add Maggi noodles and tastemaker, cook for 2-3 minutes.
  • In a pan, heat oil, sauté ½ chopped onion, add 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste, 1 chopped tomato, and 1 chopped green chili.
  • Add ½ tsp each of turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, coriander powder, and salt; cook for 2-3 minutes.
  • Mix in the cooked noodles, cook for another minute, garnish with chopped coriander leaves, and serve hot.
